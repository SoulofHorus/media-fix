CAW! CAW!

They say the Eagle Nebula looks like an Eagle, hence its name. Yet, the one thing that stands out is the color contrast. The blue and orange hues clash in a beautiful display. The colors are a reminder of Syracuse's school colors.

Like the Belt?

The Orion Nebula shows the contrast between a baby blue and hot pink. On the outer edges of the Nebula, there is some darker colors furthering increasing the contrast. Even though we can't see it, it does not mean we can admire its beauty.

Pretty Colors...

Whether this image is actually real or not does not matter. All that matters is that is definitely eye-catching. The green is the first thing most might notice, others might be the small amount of red lingering in between the green and yellow.

Rhymes with Felix, Helix!

Looks like a blue eye without a pupil right? Well, it is also one of the Nebulas that NASA's satelites have eyes on. It comes as no surprise that it looks like a souless person is looking back at you, but when you whisper to the Void, does it whisper back?

A Real Hole

On April 10th, 2019, NASA has discovered the first black hole that was photographed. The original file size was an absurd size, a few hundred thousand terabytes. Yet, the only thing they wanted to see was this image, oh and the Internet went all nuts over it.

You aren't afraid of Spiders?

Does anyone like Spiderman? It's just the opposite color scheme. Many of these color scheme are mutli-colored, but this one is different from the rest. The simple color scheme of black and red give it the omnimous feeling. Then again, most things in space are ominous.

Looks like a Person?

You can see a physical person before you right? It's not just me. The right arm looks like they are cradling something, but it looks taller than it actually feels like. The blue and white/black contrast looks baller. Can't say I'm jealous.

Seeing Red?

Not much to be said here except it is all red. Seeing all of this red might spark anger in your person. I can only see the beauty in its destruction.

The Rock on which We Live

Planet Earth, where we all live and breathe. It does throw all of us for a loop when we realize that we are just a tiny speck in the universe when it comes to the whole picture. Everything seems big on Earth, but outside our Solar system, there is much more to be had.

Opposite of Red

Thinking of the Red Nebula earlier, this one makes you feel calm. Serene. Like nothing can stop you, like nothing can take you out of your fungshaway. Something extraordinary to look at.

The Outer Home

The Galaxy that we live in. Quite the sight, wouldn't you agree? All this and we have yet to explore even a hundredth of it. Something that we may explore in time, but for now, this picture will suffice.

Our Warmth Giver

All of out life comes from our Sun. This gigant star gives us the warmth we require because without it, we would be a frozen tundra of death. The Sun is precious, but I could do without the sunburn.
